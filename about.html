My Published Article

ALİEFENDİOĞLU, Y., & ÇEVİK, T., (2015). Comparison of Loan Usage Amounts and House Sales Values ​​of Mortgage Loan Customers. Çankaya District Example. International Journal of Social Research, vol.8, no.41, 1101-1115.

In this study, housing loan applications and the infrastructure of the housing finance system in Turkey were examined. Using data on "sales-mortgage" transactions between 2010 and 2012 obtained from Çankaya Land Registry Directorate, the reasons for the differences between the loan usage amounts of housing loan customers and the declared sales values ​​of real estate were investigated. By examining the changes in the data regarding loan usage between 2010 and 2012, it is observed that loan usage amounts approach the value of the real estate and house sales values ​​are declared more realistic. In general, the main reasons why house sales values ​​are unrealistic are; It has been determined that housing loan collateral values are overstated, high fee rates and lack of awareness of loan users.
